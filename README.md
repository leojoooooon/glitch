# glitch —— symbiosis

The creative intent behind this piece stems from my exploration of audiovisual controllability.

I am deeply interested in deconstructing and examining diverse AV languages and modes of transmission—whether it is the integrated creative logic of combining Max/MSP and VCV Rack with visual programming, the semi-improvisational AV syntax of live sets and VJ/DJ performances, or the contrasting methodologies and communicative directions of electronic versus pop music. I believe that expressions shaped by different creative purposes and modalities yield vastly different outputs, even when driven by the same initial expectations.

In this work, I attempted to discard deliberate conceptual framing, fully immersing my perception within external audio and image assets. By assembling this fragmented information, I constructed an audiovisual experiment. The sound consists of elements from Freesound paired with my own custom sound design, while the imagery is sourced from Unsplash. I developed a reciprocal, back-and-forth dialogue between audio loops crafted in Ableton Live and visuals generated in TouchDesigner, avoiding any absolute "audio-first" or "visual-first" hierarchy. Ultimately, the final presentation still conveys an intuitive emotional language, and even a certain subjectivity—an outcome perhaps owed to serendipity and the unique character of the source material itself.

The operational logic of the final output leans toward a mutual progression, where visuals and sound continuously drive each other forward. This ultimate output was recorded as a single, complete live set, captured through a custom-designed TouchDesigner system and performatively shaped in real-time via hardware controllers and parameter manipulation.
